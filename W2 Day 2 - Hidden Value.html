Philosophy Day 2

The Hidden Value

"A society is judged not by its strongest members, but by how it integrates and honors the gifts of those it considers 'weak'."

Today's Focus: Appreciating the talents of PWDs and the underprivileged.

Redefining Success

In our society, who do we usually consider "successful"?

  • Is it only about wealth?
  • Is it only about physical "wholeness"?
Discussion: Why does the "Productivity Myth" make us ignore the unique contributions of those at the margins?

The Social Model of Disability

A person is "disabled" not by their impairment, but by a society that refuses to accommodate them.

Epistemic Advantage: The underprivileged often see truths about justice and community that those at the top overlook.

Talents Beyond Money

The gifts of PWDs and the poor are essential to the moral fabric of society:

  • Sensory Adaptation & Creative Problem Solving.
  • Extreme Resilience & Grit.
  • Communal Solidarity & Shared Survival.

These are not just traits; they are contributions.
