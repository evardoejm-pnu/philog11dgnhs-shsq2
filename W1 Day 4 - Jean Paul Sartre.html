Philosophy Day 4

The Character Script

"We are not born with a manual. We have to write it as we go."

Today we explore the ultimate responsibility: Inventing Yourself.

Existence Precedes Essence

Jean-Paul Sartre, a famous philosopher, argued that for human beings: Existence comes first.

  • Objects: A papercutter is designed with a purpose before it is made. Its "what it is" (essence) comes before its "being" (existence).
  • Humans: We just show up on the scene. We exist first, and then we spend our lives defining what we are.

The iPhone vs. The Human

Think about how different we are from the technology in your pocket:

  • The iPhone (Essence first): Before the phone was even made, engineers knew exactly what it would do. It was designed to call, text, and browse. It cannot choose to be a toaster tomorrow.
  • The Human (Existence first): You were born without a "settings" menu. There is no pre-programmed button for "Honest" or "Lazy." You have to decide every morning what your "features" will be.

"Condemned to be Free"

Sartre famously said humans are abandoned in the world.

No Blueprint, No Excuses:
Because there is no "Grand Design" telling you who to be, you have total freedom. But this freedom is heavy. You cannot say "I was born this way" or "I have no choice." If you are mean, it is because you choose to be mean right now. This weight of responsibility creates Anguish.

We are "condemned" to freedom because we didn't ask to be here, but now we are responsible for every stroke of our script.

Brick by Brick

Every choice is like a brick in a building. If you choose honesty once, you are a person who told the truth. If you choose it 1,000 times, you become an honest person.

Your freedom is the tool you use to build the house you eventually have to live in. Sartre calls this "The Project."

Activity 1: The Split-Screen Life

The Scenario: You find an unattended laptop in the library.

Path A: You walk away and leave it there.
Path B: You turn it in to the lost and found.

Think about Sartre's view: Which path helps you create the "Essence" of a trustworthy person? How does your character change in one month depending on the path?

Activity 2: The Two-Path Script

On your paper, write a short summary for the laptop scenario:

  • Path A (Ignored): How do you feel about your "Essence" 1 week later?
  • Path B (Returned): How did this choice change your self-respect?
  • Reflect: Which "version" of yourself do you want to live with?

Activity 3: Rewriting Yourself

Identify a trait you dislike (e.g., "I am lazy").

The Challenge:
  1. Stop saying "I AM lazy" (Treating yourself as an object).
  2. Start saying "I have CHOSEN to be lazy in the past."
  3. New Script: What specific choice will you make tomorrow to "author" a different version of yourself?
