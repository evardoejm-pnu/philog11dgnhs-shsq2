The Person and Death

The Horizon of Finitude

"To philosophize is to learn how to die." — Montaigne

Objective: Recognize how the awareness of our limited time (finitude) creates the possibility for a meaningful life.

Heidegger’s Concept

Martin Heidegger argued that humans are "Being-towards-death."

  • Death is not an "end" that happens later; it is a constant possibility here and now.
  • Awareness of death pulls us out of the "They" (Das Man)—the crowd that tells us how to live.
  • Recognizing our finitude makes our choices urgent and unique.

Living Authentically

We have two ways of facing our limited time:

Inauthentic: Distracting ourselves with trivial things to forget that we will die.
Authentic: Accepting our finitude and taking total responsibility for our choices.

Death is the "non-relational" possibility: No one can take your death away from you, just as no one can live your life for you.
