Existence Precedes Essence

Unlike a tool (like a scissors) which is made with a specific purpose, humans are born without a pre-set blueprint.

  • We first exist, and then we define our essence.
  • We are "condemned to be free" because we must choose who we are.
  • Our purpose is not found; it is constructed through action.

The Self as a Project

Sartre identifies two parts of the human condition:

  • Facticity: The unchangeable facts (your birthplace, your height, your past).
  • Transcendence: Your ability to move beyond those facts through your Projects.
A project is a commitment to a goal that gives your life direction. What project are you building right now?

Activity: The Blueprint of Projects

Written Task (5-7 Sentences):

  1. List one "Facticity" (something you cannot change about your situation).
  2. Define one "Life Project" you want to achieve (it must be a commitment, not just a wish).
  3. How does this project help you "Transcend" your facticity?
  4. If you fail at this project, would your life still have meaning? Why?
