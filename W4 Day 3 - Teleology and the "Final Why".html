The End of Action

Teleology and the "Final Why"

"All things in nature have a purpose, and humans are no exception." — Aristotle

Objective: Explain the meaning of life by exploring where our actions lead and identifying our Telos (Ultimate End).

The Concept of Telos

Teleology comes from the Greek word 'Telos', meaning "end," "goal," or "purpose."

  • Every action we perform is a "means" to an "end."
  • We study to get a degree; we get a degree to find a job; we find a job to... (Why?)
  • The "Final Why" is the ultimate goal that makes all other actions worth doing.

Where is it all leading?

Philosophers differ on what the "Final Why" is:

  • Theological: Life leads toward a union with a higher power or divine truth.
  • Humanistic: Life leads toward a legacy, contribution, or the flourishing of humanity.
  • Existential: There is no pre-set end; we must invent our own destination.

Activity: The Final Why Audit

Written Task (5-7 Sentences):

  1. Think of your most important current goal (e.g., graduating).
  2. Ask "Why?" to that goal. Then ask "Why?" to that answer.
  3. Continue until you reach a "Final Why" that cannot be explained further.
  4. How does this "Final Why" change the way you handle a bad day today?
