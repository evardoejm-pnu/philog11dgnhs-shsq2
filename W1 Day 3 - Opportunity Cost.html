Today's Focus: Understanding Trade-offs and Opportunity Costs.

The Reality of "Finitude"

Humans are limited by time and space. We cannot be everywhere at once.

  • If you choose to study for 2 hours, you cannot sleep for those same 2 hours.
  • If you buy a game, you cannot use that same money for lunch.
Discussion: Why does having limited time make our choices more valuable?

What is a Trade-off?

Opportunity Cost is the value of the "next best thing" that you gave up.

Choosing is not just about what you get; it's about what you are willing to lose to get it.

The Scholarship Dilemma

The Scenario: You are offered a part-time job that pays well but requires you to work 30 hours a week. Taking it means you will definitely lose your academic scholarship because you won't have time to study.

Reflection: What is the "Gained" and what is the "Relinquished"? Is the money now worth the degree later?
