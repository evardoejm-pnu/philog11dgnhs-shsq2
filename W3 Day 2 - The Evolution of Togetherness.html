Societal Evolution

Evolution of Togetherness

"As the way we survive changes, the way we connect must evolve."

Objective: Compare different forms of societies (Agrarian, Industrial, Virtual) and their impact on individuality.

The Agrarian Roots

In traditional farming societies, survival depends on collective ritual and shared land.

  • Mechanical Solidarity: People are held together because they are all similar.
  • Low Individuality: The community script is more important than personal choice.
In this setting, being "different" was often seen as a threat to the harvest and the village.

Industrial to Virtual

Industrial: Specialization creates "Organic Solidarity"—we depend on strangers for our needs.

Virtual: Identity becomes fluid and digital; we are connected by interest rather than geography.
