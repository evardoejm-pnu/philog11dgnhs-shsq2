Finding Meaning in Everything

Viktor Frankl, a psychiatrist and Holocaust survivor, argued that even in suffering, meaning exists.

  • Creative Value: What we give to the world (Work/Projects).
  • Experiential Value: What we take from the world (Love/Beauty).
  • Attitudinal Value: The stance we take toward unavoidable suffering.

The Three Pillars of Your Story

A meaningful life is a synthesis of what we have learned this week:

  1. Finitude: Accepting that our time is scarce and precious.
  2. Projects: Actively choosing who we are through our actions.
  3. Teleology: Aiming toward a "Final Why" that gives our life a direction.
