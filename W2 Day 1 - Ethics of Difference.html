Emmanuel Levinas & The Other

The "Other" is a mystery that can never be fully grasped or "boxed in."

  • Totalization: Imposing your own ideas and categories onto another person.
  • The Face-to-Face: An encounter that demands respect and forbids us from "killing" (reducing) the other person's true self.

Being-With Others

Intersubjectivity is not just socializing; it is a "being-with" that respects "alterity" (otherness).

Accepting differences is an active ethical commitment to let others be who they are without judgment.
