Day 1: The Ripple Effect

Unit: Freedom of the Human Person

Competency 5.1: Realize that “all actions have consequences.”

"Freedom is not the absence of consequences, but the power to choose them."

Welcome, class. Today we explore how your choices move the world.

Intro: The Decision Point

Look at the board. Imagine this scenario:

"You witness a close friend bullying a younger student."

The teacher will now draw a circle. What happens if you stay silent? What happens if you speak up? Let's map the first 'ripples'.

The Philosophy of Causality

Every human act is a "Prime Mover."

  • Natural Events: A rock falls because of gravity. It has no choice.
  • Human Acts: You move because you decide to. You are the origin of the chain.

Because you are the origin, you "own" the results that follow.

Horizontal vs. Vertical

There are two types of ripples created by your freedom:

  1. Horizontal: The outward effects (e.g., your friend gets hurt, you get a detention).
  2. Vertical: The inward effects (e.g., you become a person who lacks courage, or a person who stands for justice).

Which one stays with you longer?

Activity: The Multi-Tiered Ripple Map

Task: In pairs, choose one action (e.g., Cheating on a quiz vs. Being honest).

Draw three rings on your paper:

  • Ring 1 (Immediate): Results in the next 10 minutes.
  • Ring 2 (Relational): How it affects trust and friendships.
  • Ring 3 (Character): Who do you become after 100 choices like this?

Conclusion: The Package Deal

Remember this guiding principle for the week:

"We are free to choose our actions, but we are not free to choose the consequences."

When you pick up one end of the stick, you pick up the other end too. Action and consequence are a single package.
