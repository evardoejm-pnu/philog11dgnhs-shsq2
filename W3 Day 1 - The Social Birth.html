Philosophy of Society

The Social Birth

"We are not born with a personality; we are born into a society that hands us one."

Objective: Recognize how individuals form societies and are transformed by them.

The Looking-Glass Self

Charles Horton Cooley argued that society acts as a mirror.

  • We imagine how we appear to others.
  • We imagine their judgment of that appearance.
  • We develop our "Self" based on these reflections.

Creation & Transformation

How the social world is built and maintained:

  1. Externalization: Humans create social rules and cultures.
  2. Objectivation: Rules start to feel like unchangeable facts.
  3. Internalization: We absorb these rules into our own minds.
